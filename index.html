F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What are common mental health disorders?

    Common mental health disorders include depression, anxiety disorders (such as generalized anxiety disorder and social anxiety disorder), bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).

  • How prevalent are mental health issues?

    Mental health issues are common. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), depression is the leading cause of disability worldwide. Millions of people of all ages and backgrounds experience mental health challenges.

  • What are the signs of a mental health problem?

    Signs can vary, but common indicators include persistent sadness, excessive worry, changes in sleep or appetite, withdrawal from social activities, mood swings, and difficulty concentrating.

  • Can mental health issues be treated?

    Yes, mental health issues are treatable. Effective treatments may include therapy (such as cognitive-behavioral therapy or counseling) and/or medication. Support from friends and family is also essential.

  • How can I support someone with a mental health problem?

    Offer a listening ear, express empathy, avoid judgment, encourage them to seek professional help, and educate yourself about their condition. Be patient and understanding.

  • What is the difference between sadness and depression?

    Sadness is a normal emotional response to specific events and tends to be temporary. Depression, on the other hand, is a persistent and pervasive mood disorder that may require treatment.
